# Paper Review Rubric For Contrastive Pairing

Use this rubric for each single paper before pairwise comparison.

## Required Single-Paper Review Fields

- Contribution summary: anonymized, no title or author.
- Problem importance and positioning.
- Claimed novelty and A+B incrementality.
- Technical soundness.
- Claim-evidence alignment.
- Mechanism, theory, or causal explanation.
- Experiments, baselines, tuning fairness, and ablations.
- Dataset, metric, and evaluation setting validity.
- Reproducibility and implementation detail.
- Limitations, scope control, and overclaim risk.
- Writing clarity and reviewer readability.
- Decision-impact weaknesses.

## Review Rules

- Mark missing facts as `not reported`.
- Distinguish observed paper evidence from reviewer inference.
- Do not mention paper identity.
- Do not infer quality from source folder labels alone.
- During single-paper review, do not expose or use high/general source labels. Labels may be revealed only after both papers in a pair have completed blind reviews.
- Keep the review focused on patterns that can later support reviewer taste inference.
